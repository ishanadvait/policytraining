Fundamental Concepts

Key terminologies and definitions

Data Privacy vs. Data Protection

üîí
Data Privacy

The right of individuals to control how their personal information is collected, used, and shared. It focuses on the "what" and "why."

üõ°Ô∏è
Data Protection

The mechanisms, policies, and practices used to safeguard personal data from unauthorized access, disclosure, or misuse. It focuses on the "how."

What is Personal Data?

üìã Personal Data Definition

Any information that relates to an identified or identifiable individual. This includes:

  • Direct Identifiers: Name, email address, phone number, social security number, Aadhaar
  • Indirect Identifiers: Date of birth, location data, IP address, device ID
  • Digital Identifiers: Online identifiers, cookies, digital footprints

Sensitive Personal Data

‚ö†Ô∏è Requires Higher Protection

Sensitive data includes:

  • Passwords
  • Financial information (bank accounts, credit cards)
  • Health information
  • Sexual orientation
  • Biometric data
  • Genetic data
  • Religious or political beliefs
  • Caste or tribe information

Key Terminologies

üë§
Data Subject / Data Principal

The individual whose personal data is being collected or processed

üè¢
Data Controller / Data Fiduciary

The organization that determines the purposes and means of processing personal data

‚öôÔ∏è
Data Processor

An entity that processes data on behalf of the controller/fiduciary

‚úÖ
Consent

A clear, informed, and voluntary agreement from the data subject to process their data

GDPR Overview

The global gold standard in data protection

What is GDPR?

üá™üá∫ General Data Protection Regulation

Adopted by the European Union in 2016 and effective since May 25, 2018. It is one of the most comprehensive and influential data protection laws globally. It sets a high standard for data protection, influencing legislation worldwide, including India's DPDP Act.

When Does GDPR Apply to You?

üåç Extraterritorial Scope

GDPR applies to organizations outside the EU if they:

  • Offer goods or services (paid or free) to EU residents
  • Monitor the behavior of EU residents
  • Important for Indian Companies: If you have EU customers, process EU employee data, or have an EU subsidiary, GDPR likely applies to you!

India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023

India's comprehensive data privacy legislation

Background and Objectives

üáÆüá≥ The DPDP Act, 2023

Passed by the Indian Parliament on August 11, 2023. It marks a significant step in India's journey towards a comprehensive data protection framework. Objectives include:

  • Establishing rights for individuals (Data Principals) regarding their personal data
  • Defining duties and obligations of organizations processing personal data (Data Fiduciaries)
  • Setting up a regulatory body ‚Äì Data Protection Board of India

Applicability

üáÆüá≥
Within India

Processing of digital personal data collected within India (online or digitized offline)

üåç
Outside India

Processing of data outside India if related to offering goods/services in India

Key Definitions

  • Data Principal: The individual whose data is being processed (similar to GDPR's "data subject")
  • Data Fiduciary: The entity determining purpose and means of processing (similar to GDPR's "controller")
  • Significant Data Fiduciary: A Data Fiduciary notified by the Government based on volume/sensitivity of data, risk to data principals, impact on sovereignty, etc.

Consent - The Cornerstone

‚úÖ Consent Requirements

Consent must be:

  • Free: Given voluntarily, without coercion or undue pressure
  • Specific: For a particular, identified purpose
  • Informed: Data Principal must know what they are consenting to
  • Unconditional: No conditions attached (except providing the service itself)
  • Unambiguous: Clear affirmative action (affirmative opt-in)
  • Capable of Withdrawal: Can be withdrawn as easily as given

Data Protection Principles

Core principles guiding data handling

üéØ
Purpose Limitation

Data can only be processed for the specific, lawful purpose for which consent was obtained or for other lawful purposes under the Act

üìä
Data Minimization

Collect only personal data that is necessary and adequate for the specified purpose. Avoid collecting excessive data.

‚úì
Data Accuracy

Ensure personal data is accurate, complete, and kept up to date. Inaccurate data must be corrected or erased.

‚è∞
Storage Limitation

Retain data only for the duration necessary to fulfill the stated purpose. Delete or anonymize data once it's no longer needed.

üîê
Reasonable Security

Implement organizational and technical measures to protect data from breaches, including encryption, access controls, and regular audits.

üìù
Accountability

Be able to demonstrate compliance with the law, maintain records of processing activities, and respond to requests.

Individual rights under the DPDP Act

üìÑ
Right to Access

Obtain information about what data is being processed, how, and for what purpose. Request a summary of personal data and processing activities.

‚úèÔ∏è

Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data. Request erasure of data no longer needed.

‚¨ÖÔ∏è

Withdraw previously given consent at any time. Withdrawal should be as easy as giving consent.

‚öñÔ∏è

üë§

Duties of the Data Principal

üìã Your Responsibilities
  • Provide accurate, complete, and up-to-date information when submitting data
  • Not impersonate others or submit false information
  • Not file frivolous or malicious complaints
  • Comply with applicable laws when exercising rights

Data Fiduciary Obligations

Responsibilities of organizations processing data

General Obligations

üìã All Data Fiduciaries Must:
  • Obtain Consent: Prior to processing personal data (unless lawful ground exists)
  • Provide Notice: Clear, accessible notice about what data is collected, purposes, and rights
  • Implement Security: Reasonable safeguards to prevent breaches
  • Report Breaches: Notify Data Protection Board and affected Data Principals in case of personal data breach
  • Accuracy: Ensure data is complete and accurate
  • Retention Limits: Delete data when purpose is served/consent withdrawn
  • Grievance Redressal: Publish details of a grievance officer

Significant Data Fiduciary - Additional Obligations

‚ö†Ô∏è Enhanced Requirements
  • Appoint a Data Protection Officer (DPO): Based in India, point of contact for grievances
  • Appoint an Independent Data Auditor: Conduct periodic audits of data processing activities
  • Conduct Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA): For high-risk processing activities

Personal Data Breach Response

üö® Breach Notification

In case of a personal data breach:

  • Notify the Data Protection Board of India
  • Notify affected Data Principals
  • Notification should be in prescribed form and manner
  • Within prescribed timelines (to be notified by Government)

Cross-Border Data Transfers

Rules for transferring data internationally

DPDP Act Approach

üåç Transfer Rules
  • General Permission: Personal data may be transferred outside India by default
  • Restricted Countries: Government may notify specific countries/territories to which transfer is prohibited
  • "Blacklist" Approach: Unlike GDPR's "adequacy" decisions, India uses a negative list

GDPR Approach (For Reference)

  • Adequacy Decisions: Transfers allowed to countries deemed "adequate" by EU Commission
  • Safeguards: Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs), Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs)
  • Derogations: Explicit consent, contract performance, etc.

Your Role in Data Protection

Practical steps for everyday compliance

Best Practices for Every Employee

‚úÖ Daily Data Protection Habits
  • Know Your Data: Understand what personal data you handle in your role
  • Collect Minimally: Only collect data that is absolutely necessary
  • Secure Your Workspace: Lock screens, use strong passwords, secure physical documents
  • Be Cautious with Email: Verify recipients, encrypt sensitive attachments
  • Report Incidents: Immediately report any suspected data breach or security incident
  • Respect Access Controls: Only access data you need for your job
  • Verify Requests: Be wary of phishing; verify identity before sharing data
  • Follow Data Retention: Delete data when no longer needed
  • Stay Updated: Attend training and keep up with policy updates

Handling Data Subject Requests

  • Acknowledge Promptly: Respond to requests without undue delay
  • Verify Identity: Ensure the requester is who they claim to be
  • Forward Appropriately: Escalate to your Data Protection Officer or legal team
  • Document Everything: Maintain records of requests and actions taken

If You Suspect a Data Breach

üö® Immediate Actions
  1. Do NOT panic or try to cover it up
  2. Immediately notify your manager and IT/Security team
  3. Preserve evidence ‚Äì do not delete emails, logs, or files
  4. Document what happened, when, and what data may be affected
  5. Cooperate fully with the investigation
