Introduction - Why POSH Matters

Understanding the importance of a harassment-free workplace

What is the POSH Act?

üìú The Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace Act, 2013

A landmark legislation in India, stemming from the Vishaka Guidelines laid down by the Supreme Court in 1997. It provides protection against sexual harassment of women at the workplace and establishes mechanisms for prevention and redressal.

Understanding Sexual Harassment

Definition, forms, and key concepts

Definition of Sexual Harassment (Section 2(n))

üìã Sexual harassment includes any unwelcome act or behavior such as:
  • Physical contact and advances
  • A demand or request for sexual favors
  • Making sexually colored remarks
  • Showing pornography
  • Any other unwelcome physical, verbal, or non-verbal conduct of sexual nature

Key Elements: "Unwelcome" and "Sexual Nature"

üö´
Unwelcome

The conduct is unsolicited and offensive to the recipient. Consent is absent or withdrawn. The perception of the recipient is crucial.

‚ö†Ô∏è
Sexual Nature

The conduct must have a sexual connotation or implication.

Forms of Sexual Harassment

üó£Ô∏è Verbal Harassment

  • Offensive jokes or comments with sexual undertones
  • Sexually suggestive remarks, innuendos, or teasing
  • Spreading rumors about someone's sex life
  • Derogatory or condescending comments based on gender
  • Unwelcome compliments about appearance
  • Demands or requests for sexual favors

üëÅÔ∏è Non-Verbal Harassment

  • Staring or leering
  • Displaying sexually suggestive objects, pictures, or posters
  • Sexual gestures or body language
  • Whistling or catcalling
  • Sending unwanted explicit emails, messages, or social media content
  • Online harassment (cyber-sexual harassment)

‚úã Physical Harassment

  • Unwelcome touching, patting, pinching, brushing against
  • Blocking a person's path
  • Assault or molestation
  • Physical advances or gestures

üíº Quid Pro Quo Harassment

‚ö†Ô∏è "This for That" Harassment

Directly or indirectly threatening or promising benefits in exchange for sexual favors:

  • "If you do this for me, you'll get that promotion"
  • "If you don't, you'll lose your job/opportunity"
  • Implied threats about present or future employment status

Hostile Work Environment

üè¢ Creating an Intimidating Environment

Occurs when unwelcome conduct of a sexual nature is severe or pervasive enough to create an intimidating, hostile, or offensive working environment. It doesn't necessarily involve a direct demand for sexual favors but impacts the person's ability to work.

Examples: Continuous sexist jokes, display of inappropriate material, constant unwelcome comments, gender-based bullying.

The POSH Act, 2013 - Key Provisions

Understanding the legal framework

Objectives of the Act

üõ°Ô∏è
Prevention

To prevent acts of sexual harassment

üö´
Prohibition

To prohibit any acts of sexual harassment

‚öñÔ∏è
Redressal

To provide mechanism for complaint redressal

Key Provisions

  • Mandatory Policy: Every employer must formulate and disseminate an internal policy on sexual harassment
  • Internal Complaints Committee (ICC): Mandated in every organization with 10+ employees
  • Local Complaints Committee (LCC): For organizations with <10 employees or complaints against the employer
  • Complaint Mechanism: Clear process for filing and addressing complaints
  • Inquiry Process: Specified procedure ensuring natural justice
  • Interim Relief: Provisions during inquiry pendency
  • Conciliation: Option for non-monetary conciliation before inquiry
  • Penalties: For non-compliance by employer and malicious complaints

What Constitutes a "Workplace"? (Section 2(o))

üè¢ Very Broad Definition

The workplace includes:

  • Any department, organization, undertaking, establishment, office
  • Private sector organizations, NGOs, trusts, societies
  • Any place visited during the course of employment:
    • Transportation provided by the employer
    • Client meetings, conferences, training sessions
    • Company events, off-site locations
    • Virtual workplaces (official emails, video calls, team chats)

Applicability - Who is Covered?

Understanding who is protected and who can be a respondent

Who is Protected? "Aggrieved Woman" (Section 2(a))

üë© The Act protects any woman, of any age, whether employed or not

This includes:

  • Regular, temporary, ad-hoc, contractual, daily wage employees
  • Workers, probationers, trainees, apprentices
  • Interns and volunteers
  • Students
  • Clients or customers visiting the workplace
  • Domestic workers
  • Anyone entering the workplace

Who Can Be a Respondent?

The person against whom the complaint is made can be:

  • Any employee (senior, peer, junior)
  • A manager or supervisor
  • A director or board member
  • A client, vendor, or customer
  • Temporary staff, consultant, intern, or visitor
  • Any person who commits an act of sexual harassment at the workplace

Applicability Threshold

üìä When Does the Act Apply?
  • 10+ Employees: Mandatory constitution of ICC
  • <10 Employees: Complaint to Local Complaints Committee (LCC)
  • Complaint against employer: Goes to LCC at district level

Internal Complaints Committee (ICC)

Structure, composition, and functions

ICC Composition (Section 4)

üë©‚Äçüíº
Presiding Officer

A woman employed at a senior level in the organization

üë•
Internal Members

Minimum 2 members from amongst employees, preferably committed to women's causes

üèõÔ∏è
External Member

One member from NGOs or associations committed to women's causes

‚öñÔ∏è
Composition Rules

Minimum 4 members, at least 50% must be women

Key ICC Details

  • Term: Members hold office for up to 3 years
  • Quorum: Minimum 3 members including Presiding Officer
  • Independence: Members must act impartially and without bias

Role and Functions of the ICC

üìã ICC Responsibilities
  • Receive Complaints: Receive and acknowledge complaints of sexual harassment
  • Conduct Inquiry: Conduct fair, impartial, and timely inquiry
  • Recommend Action: Recommend appropriate actions based on findings
  • Conciliation: Facilitate conciliation if requested (non-monetary only)
  • Awareness: Assist in organizing workshops and awareness programs
  • Annual Report: Submit annual report to District Officer and employer

Complaint Mechanism

How to file a complaint

Who Can File a Complaint?

  • The Aggrieved Woman: The woman subjected to sexual harassment
  • In Case of Incapacity: Her legal heir or any prescribed person can file on her behalf

Where to File?

üè¢
ICC

Internal Complaints Committee of your organization

üèõÔ∏è
LCC

Local Complaints Committee if organization has <10 employees or complaint is against employer

Time Limit for Filing (Section 9)

‚è∞ Important Deadlines
  • Complaint must be filed within 3 months from the date of incident
  • For a series of incidents, within 3 months from the last incident
  • ICC/LCC may extend by another 3 months if circumstances prevented timely filing

What Should a Complaint Include?

üìù Complaint Requirements
  • Must be in writing
  • Name and contact details of the aggrieved woman
  • Name of the respondent
  • Date(s) and time(s) of the incident(s)
  • Description of the incident(s) with specific details
  • Names of any witnesses (if applicable)
  • Supporting documents or evidence (if any)
  • Six copies of the complaint with supporting documents

Confidentiality (Section 16)

üîí Strict Confidentiality Required

The identity of the aggrieved woman, respondent, and witnesses, as well as the details of the inquiry, must be kept confidential. Publication of identity or information could lead to a penalty.

Inquiry Process & Timelines

Step-by-step process and key deadlines

Step-by-Step Process

üìã Step 1: Receiving the Complaint (Within 7 working days)
  • ICC receives the written complaint
  • If not in writing, Presiding Officer assists in documentation
  • Copy provided to respondent within 7 working days
üìã Step 2: Response from Respondent (Within 10 working days)
  • Respondent submits written response
  • Including supporting documents and witness names
üìã Step 3: Conciliation (Optional - Section 10)
  • At the request of the aggrieved woman, conciliation may be attempted
  • Important: Conciliation should NOT involve monetary settlement
  • If successful, settlement is recorded and forwarded to employer
üìã Step 4: Inquiry Proceedings (Within 90 days - Section 11)
  • If conciliation fails or is not opted for, inquiry proceeds
  • Conducted according to principles of natural justice
  • Both parties have fair opportunity to be heard
  • Right to present case, examine witnesses, produce documents
  • Right to cross-examine
  • No legal practitioners allowed in inquiry proceedings
  • ICC has powers similar to a Civil Court
üìã Step 5: Inquiry Report (Within 10 days of completion - Section 13)
  • ICC prepares Inquiry Report with findings and recommendations
  • If allegations proven: recommends action against respondent
  • If allegations not proven: recommends no action
  • If complaint is malicious/false: may recommend action against complainant
üìã Step 6: Employer's Action (Within 60 days - Section 13)
  • Employer must act on ICC recommendations within 60 days
  • Actions may include: disciplinary action, compensation, transfer, etc.
  • If employer disagrees, must record reasons in writing

Interim Relief (Section 12)

üõ°Ô∏è Protection During Inquiry

At the request of the aggrieved woman, ICC may recommend:

  • Transfer of the aggrieved woman or respondent
  • Grant leave up to 3 months (additional to regular entitlement)
  • Restrain respondent from reporting on/evaluating/supervising the aggrieved woman
  • Any other appropriate relief

Roles & Responsibilities

Understanding everyone's role in prevention

Employer's Responsibilities (Section 19)

üè¢ Employer Must:
  • Constitute ICC: Ensure proper formation with correct composition and training
  • Formulate Policy: Develop and widely disseminate anti-sexual harassment policy
  • Provide Safe Environment: Take all necessary steps to prevent harassment
  • Organize Awareness: Conduct regular workshops and sensitization programs
  • Display Information: Prominently display penal consequences and ICC details
  • Provide Assistance: Extend all necessary facilities to ICC
  • Act on Recommendations: Implement ICC recommendations within stipulated time
  • Monitor & Report: Submit annual report to District Officer

ICC Responsibilities

  • Maintain strict confidentiality throughout the process
  • Conduct fair, impartial, and timely inquiries
  • Provide a safe and supportive environment for complainants
  • Act within the powers granted by the Act
  • Submit accurate and timely inquiry reports
  • Educate employees and assist in prevention

Consequences & Prevention Measures

Understanding penalties and proactive prevention

Consequences for the Respondent (If Proven)

‚ö†Ô∏è Disciplinary Actions Include:
  • Written apology
  • Warning, reprimand, or censure
  • Withholding of promotion, increment, or pay raise
  • Termination from service (for severe cases)
  • Transfer
  • Community service or counseling
  • Deduction from salary to pay compensation
  • Criminal charges under IPC for severe cases (molestation, assault)

Consequences for the Employer (Non-Compliance - Section 26)

üí∞
Initial Fine

Up to INR 50,000 for first violation

üí∏
Repeated Violation

Fine can be doubled + license/registration may be cancelled

Consequences for Malicious Complaints (Section 14)

‚öñÔ∏è Important Safeguards

If ICC concludes that the complaint was made maliciously or with false intent, or that false evidence was provided knowingly:

  • Action may be recommended against the complainant
  • However: A mere inability to substantiate a complaint is NOT considered malicious intent
  • This provision is to prevent false accusations, not to deter genuine complaints

Prevention Measures - Your Role

‚úÖ Be Proactive
  • Be Aware: Understand what constitutes sexual harassment and recognize red flags
  • Speak Up: Clearly communicate if behavior is unwelcome; report incidents
  • Be an Ally: If you witness harassment, consider intervening safely or reporting
  • Treat Everyone with Respect: Be mindful of your words, actions, and non-verbal cues
  • Respect Personal Space: Be conscious of physical boundaries
  • Avoid Inappropriate Jokes: Refrain from sexist, homophobic, or derogatory jokes
  • Maintain Professionalism Online: Apply same standards to emails, chats, and social media
  • Ask for Consent: Always seek explicit consent for physical contact or personal conversations
  • Lead by Example: Managers must role model appropriate behavior
